# Y-PIE Research Program

Y-PIE includes research questions that must remain separate from production guarantees.

## Research tracks

### R1 — Artistic intelligence
Evaluate whether multimodal ensembles can produce stable, useful and personally calibrated assessments of composition, abstraction, symbolism, originality and curatorial value.

### R2 — Emotional and biographical significance
Identify observable proxies, uncertainty limits and calibration methods without presenting machine estimates as personal truth.

### R3 — Visual DNA
Develop explainable trait discovery, temporal evolution, creative-period detection and project-specific visual languages.

### R4 — Corpus-relative originality
Measure novelty relative to the user's library while distinguishing meaningful innovation from noise or model artifacts.

### R5 — Series and lineage
Detect variants, families, influences, transitions, mutations and bridge images across projects and time.

### R6 — Multimodal knowledge fusion
Link visual evidence to conversations, documents, events, places, projects and sensor context through KAP.

### R7 — Safe pruning
Model redundancy and utility expiration while minimizing false positives on unique, emotional or historically meaningful assets.

### R8 — Curatorial intelligence
Generate coherent collections, exhibitions, books and narratives with transparent selection rationales.

### R9 — Evaluation science
Build user-specific benchmark sets, longitudinal calibration metrics and disagreement-aware model evaluation.

## Research artifact requirements

Each experiment must record:
- hypothesis;
- dataset and sampling method;
- models and versions;
- prompt/policy versions;
- metrics;
- qualitative failures;
- privacy constraints;
- cost and hardware;
- reproducibility instructions;
- decision impact.

Research outputs do not become production behavior until accepted through ADR/RFC and regression testing.
