# Y-PIE Vision and Principles

## Vision

Y-PIE is the visual cognitive system of Y-OS: a system that perceives visual reality, converts it into structured knowledge, relates it to the rest of the user's life and work, and supports reasoning, memory, creativity and action.

Its purpose is not to classify files. Its purpose is to make visual experience cognitively available.

## Strategic outcomes

Y-PIE must enable Y-OS to:

- understand the contents and context of images;
- distinguish art, memory, utility, evidence, inspiration and noise;
- identify people, places, events, objects, styles and concepts;
- estimate technical, artistic, emotional and biographical value separately;
- discover patterns across time and projects;
- reveal a personal visual language;
- build and maintain a Visual DNA model;
- contribute to a cross-modal Personal Knowledge Genome;
- support curation, narrative, search and pruning without unsafe autonomy.

## Founding principles

### 1. Knowledge, not isolated media
Every visual asset must be transformable into linked knowledge objects in KAP.

### 2. KAP owns canonical knowledge
Y-PIE produces observations, interpretations and relations. KAP owns the canonical graph, identity resolution and cross-modal truth layer.

### 3. Evidence before assertion
Every machine inference must retain source asset, model, prompt, version, timestamp, confidence and supporting evidence.

### 4. Multi-dimensional value
Technical quality, aesthetic strength, artistic significance, emotional value, biographical value, utility and uniqueness are separate dimensions.

### 5. Personal calibration
Generic models provide priors. User feedback creates the personal model.

### 6. Reversible automation
Early automation organizes and recommends. Destructive actions require quarantine, explicit rules and human validation.

### 7. Local-first where practical
Bulk analysis, embeddings, OCR, color, quality and duplicate detection should run locally when feasible. Cloud reasoning is reserved for high-value or ambiguous cases.

### 8. Progressive intelligence
The architecture must support a small MVP and evolve toward deep creative intelligence without redesigning the foundations.

### 9. Cross-modal continuity
Visual knowledge must connect to documents, conversations, projects, places, calendar events, sensors and other KAP modalities.

### 10. Human meaning outranks machine neatness
A technically poor but emotionally unique image must be preserved over a technically excellent but redundant image.

## Non-goals

Y-PIE is not intended to:

- replace original media storage;
- make irreversible deletion decisions autonomously;
- claim objective truth about artistic merit;
- infer sensitive personal facts without explicit governance;
- create a second knowledge graph outside KAP;
- bind the system permanently to Immich or any single model vendor.
