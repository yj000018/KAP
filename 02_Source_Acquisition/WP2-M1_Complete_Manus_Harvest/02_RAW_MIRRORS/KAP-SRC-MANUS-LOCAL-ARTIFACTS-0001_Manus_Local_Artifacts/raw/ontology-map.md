# ELYSIUM: Ontology Map

This document defines the structural architecture of the ELYSIUM civilizational ontology.

---

## 1. Three Scales (Provisional)
*Note: Final names pending Founder confirmation.*

1. **Individual / Micro**: The scale of personal agency, consciousness, and local metabolic interaction.
2. **Collective / Meso**: The scale of community, institutions, organizations, and regional cohesion.
3. **Civilizational / Macro**: The planetary scale of global infrastructure, biosphere interaction, and universal governance.

---

## 2. Seven Foundations

The core structural pillars of the civilizational metabolism.

### F01 — Material Base
- **Definition**: The physical infrastructure, resources, and spatial organization of civilization.
- **Function**: Processes matter and energy to sustain physical existence.
- **Current Pathology**: Linear extraction, resource depletion, infrastructural fragility.
- **Regenerative Direction**: Circular metabolism, renewable energy, resilient habitats.

### F02 — Vitality
- **Definition**: The biological and ecological health of the population and the biosphere.
- **Function**: Sustains life, regenerates ecosystems, and ensures physical well-being.
- **Current Pathology**: Ecological degradation, systemic disease, separation from nature.
- **Regenerative Direction**: Biosphere regeneration, holistic health, symbiotic agriculture.

### F03 — Agency
- **Definition**: The mechanisms of value creation, work, innovation, and economic exchange.
- **Function**: Mobilizes human potential and coordinates productive action.
- **Current Pathology**: Financialization, alienation from labor, extractive economics.
- **Regenerative Direction**: Purpose-driven enterprise, regenerative finance, distributed production.

### F04 — Cohesion
- **Definition**: The social fabric, relationships, culture, and sense of belonging.
- **Function**: Binds individuals into functional, resilient collectives.
- **Current Pathology**: Atomization, polarization, cultural fragmentation.
- **Regenerative Direction**: Deep community, relational wealth, unified cultural narratives.

### F05 — Governance
- **Definition**: The systems of law, justice, decision-making, and institutional order.
- **Function**: Regulates flows, resolves conflicts, and directs collective action.
- **Current Pathology**: Institutional sclerosis, democratic deficit, short-termism.
- **Regenerative Direction**: Anticipatory governance, distributed justice, systemic transparency.

### F06 — Vision
- **Definition**: The domains of science, education, foresight, and technological direction.
- **Function**: Maps reality, transfers knowledge, and projects future possibilities.
- **Current Pathology**: Reductionist science, utilitarian education, technological capture.
- **Regenerative Direction**: Systems thinking, wisdom-based education, technology in service of life.

### F07 — Consciousness
- **Definition**: The underlying worldview, ethics, spirituality, and meaning-making structures.
- **Function**: Provides the "why" of civilization; the ultimate orienting framework.
- **Current Pathology**: Meaning crisis, nihilism, materialist reductionism.
- **Regenerative Direction**: Integrated worldview, transcendent purpose, civilizational maturity.

## 4. 12-Step Universal Analysis Matrix (Provisional)
*Note: Final steps pending Founder confirmation.*

Every facet and foundation is analyzed through this universal metabolic sequence:

1. **Definition**: What is this domain?
2. **Function**: What is its role in the civilizational metabolism?
3. **Current State**: Where does it stand today?
4. **Historical Evolution**: How did it become this way?
5. **Core Pathologies**: What are its structural failures?
6. **Feedback Loops**: How do these failures reinforce themselves or others?
7. **Dependencies**: What does this domain rely upon?
8. **Cross-Foundation Couplings**: How does it interact with the other 6 Foundations?
9. **Leverage Points**: Where are the critical nodes for intervention?
10. **Regenerative Alternatives**: What does a healthy architecture look like?
11. **Transition Pathways**: How do we move from the current to the regenerative state?
12. **Metrics / Signals of Health**: How do we measure success?
