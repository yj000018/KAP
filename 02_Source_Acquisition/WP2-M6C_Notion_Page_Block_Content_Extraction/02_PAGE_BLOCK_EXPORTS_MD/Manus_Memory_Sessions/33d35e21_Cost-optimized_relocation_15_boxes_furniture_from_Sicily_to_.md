## Executive Summary

User sought the most economical way to move 15 boxes, luggage, and furniture from Castelmola, Sicily to Haute-Nendaz, Switzerland. Manus conducted comprehensive research comparing postal services, freight options, palletization strategies, and creative alternatives. After detailed cost simulations including customs fees, identified man-with-van service as optimal solution at €1,400-1,600, saving nearly €2,000 compared to traditional freight (€3,580).

## Key Decisions & Validations

- Eliminated furniture from cost optimization to focus on boxes and luggage
- Identified palletization as more cost-effective than individual shipping

- Selected man-with-van service as optimal balance of cost and convenience

## Lessons Learned

Worked well:

- Real price simulations provided accurate cost comparisons
- Palletization strategy significantly reduced transport costs
- Creative solutions like man-with-van services offered best value
Failed / suboptimal:

- Initial focus on traditional freight was unnecessarily expensive
- Postal services seemed cheaper but customs fees made them complex
Discoveries:

- Swiss customs fees add significant hidden costs to postal shipments
- Local palletization services available near departure point
- Man-with-van services can be 55% cheaper than traditional freight

## Challenges & Blockers

- Complex Swiss customs procedures and fees
- Weight and size limitations of postal services
- Need for precise cost calculations across multiple variables
