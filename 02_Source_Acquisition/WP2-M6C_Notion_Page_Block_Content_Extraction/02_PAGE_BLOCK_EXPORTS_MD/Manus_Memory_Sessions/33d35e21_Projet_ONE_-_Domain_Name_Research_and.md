## Executive Summary

Comprehensive domain name research for project ONE, exploring various formats (one.xxx, ONE-xxx.com, O-N-E.xxx). Discovered most desired domains are premium-priced or taken. Successfully registered O-N-E.space at promotional price of $0.98 for first year ($32.48 renewal). Explored 18 O-N-E extensions with 4 confirmed available.


## Context & Intent

User needed domain names for project ONE (integrative vision project documented in whitepaper). Goal was to find available, affordable domains aligned with project's spiritual/technological vision.


## What Was Done

Systematic research across multiple domain formats: one.xxx extensions, creative combinations (ONE-Rise.com, ONEverse.com), alternative spellings (O-N-E), and extension.one format. Verified availability and pricing through WHOIS, Namecheap, and web scraping. Analyzed alignment with ONE project vision.

- [domain list] 39 one.xxx extensions — Initial list of available extensions for 'one' domain
- [domain selection] 8 confirmed domains — Curated list including one.institute, ONE-Rise.com, ONEverse.com variants

## Key Decisions & Validations

- Rejected premium domains due to excessive pricing ($4,000-$14,000/year)
- Selected O-N-E.space as primary domain for 9/10 vision alignment and exceptional pricing
- Focused on creative variations rather than obvious one.xxx domains
- Prioritized extensions with tech/innovation connotations (.io, .ai, .space)

## Lessons Learned

Worked well:

- Multi-format approach revealed creative alternatives
- WHOIS verification provided accurate availability data
- Promotional pricing discovery on .space extension
Failed / suboptimal:

- Initial focus on one.xxx revealed all quality domains are premium
- WHOIS limitations for newer/exotic extensions
- Automated Namecheap access blocked by security
Discoveries:

- Domain squatting is extensive for 3-letter words like 'one'
- O-N-E format with hyphens creates unique brandable alternatives
- Extension .space has exceptional promotional pricing but standard renewal rates

## Challenges & Blockers

- Premium pricing on all desirable one.xxx domains (40-700x standard rates)
- Most creative combinations already registered by domain investors
- Technical limitations with automated domain verification
- WHOIS database gaps for newer TLD extensions

## Open Questions

- Additional O-N-E extensions availability on Namecheap beyond WHOIS verification
- Long-term branding strategy with O-N-E.space vs traditional extensions
- Potential for acquiring premium domains through negotiation
