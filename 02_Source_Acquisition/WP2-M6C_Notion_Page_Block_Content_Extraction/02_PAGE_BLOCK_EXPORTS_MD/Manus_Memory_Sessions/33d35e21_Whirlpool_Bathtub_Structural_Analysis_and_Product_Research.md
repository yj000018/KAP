## Executive Summary

Comprehensive analysis of whirlpool bathtub installation including precise weight calculations, structural load assessment, floor reinforcement requirements, and detailed product comparison. Initial weight estimate of 1,470 kg was challenged and corrected to 674 kg through cross-referencing multiple manufacturer data. Project scope evolved from large 180x150 cm bathtub to single-person 180x90 cm models optimized for therapeutic massage, with discovery that existing certified floor structure eliminates reinforcement needs for lighter models.


## Context & Intent

User planning to install whirlpool bathtub on wooden floor above kitchen, requiring precise structural load calculations to avoid floor damage. Critical constraint: existing wooden beam structure with wide spacing (75-150 cm). Initial focus on large bathtub shifted to single-person therapeutic massage models to reduce structural requirements and costs.


## What Was Done

Conducted parallel research across 18+ whirlpool manufacturers, verified weight specifications through geometric calculations and technical manuals, analyzed structural load requirements, created detailed floor reinforcement plans with cost estimates, compared energy consumption scenarios (heating vs refilling), and produced comprehensive product comparison of top therapeutic massage models with Swiss delivery options.

## Key Decisions & Validations

- Corrected initial weight estimate from 1,470 kg to 674 kg based on manufacturer cross-reference
- Shifted from 180x150 cm to 180x90 cm bathtubs to reduce structural load and costs
- Identified that existing certified floor structure eliminates need for reinforcement with lighter models
- Selected Hafro Mode 180x90 as optimal choice for therapeutic massage (2.45 kW, 286 kg)
- Determined refilling strategy saves 6,158 CHF/year vs continuous heating

## Lessons Learned

Worked well:

- Parallel manufacturer research provided comprehensive cross-validation
- Geometric verification of weight claims revealed realistic estimates
- Color-coded architectural plans facilitated clear technical communication
- Discovery of existing certified structure dramatically simplified project
Failed / suboptimal:

- Initial weight estimates from single sources proved significantly inflated
- Some manufacturer specifications missing from official documentation
- Power consumption data inconsistently reported across models
Discoveries:

- Existing 180x80 cm bathtub certification means no reinforcement needed for similar/lighter models
- Therapeutic massage requires minimum 2.0 kW power for effective treatment
- Swiss delivery logistics add significant complexity and cost to procurement

## Challenges & Blockers

- Inconsistent technical specifications across manufacturer websites
- Missing power ratings for several major models requiring direct manufacturer contact
- Wide structural beam spacing (75-150 cm) vs standard 40-60 cm creates load concentration issues
- Swiss import logistics and delivery coordination for specialized products

## Open Questions

- Confirmation of exact weights for Hafro Mode (286 kg) and Riho Lugo (298 kg) models
- Power ratings for therapeutic massage capability verification
- Swiss delivery timelines and import requirements
- Installation requirements for electrical connections (2.5-4.0 kW power supply)
